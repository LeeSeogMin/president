## About the EGDI
The E-Government Development Index (EGDI) is a composite measure of three dimensions:
- **OSI** (Online Service Index): Scope and quality of online services
- **TII** (Telecommunication Infrastructure Index): Adequacy of telecom infrastructure
- **HCI** (Human Capital Index): Inherent human capital

EGDI = (OSI + TII + HCI) / 3. Scores range from 0 to 1. Published biennially by the UN.

## Key Observations

1. **Peak ranking (Rank 1)**: South Korea held the global #1 position for three consecutive survey periods -- 2010, 2012, and 2014.
2. **Consistent top-tier performance**: Never ranked below 6th since 2008, and never below 13th across all survey years.
3. **Online services leadership**: Achieved a perfect OSI score of 1.0000 in four survey years (2010, 2012, 2020, 2024).
4. **Steady improvement in TII**: Telecom infrastructure index rose from 0.6390 (2010) to 0.9917 (2024), reflecting massive broadband and mobile expansion.
5. **HCI decline trend**: Human Capital Index has gradually declined from 0.9929 (2010) to 0.9120 (2024), likely reflecting methodological changes and relative gains by other countries.
6. **2024 ranking**: Ranked 4th globally (behind Denmark, Estonia, and Singapore) with an EGDI of 0.9679.

## Notes
- Survey years 2003, 2004, and 2005 were published annually; from 2008 onward, surveys are biennial.
- No survey was published in 2006 or 2007.
- Sub-index values for earlier years (2003-2005) may use slightly different methodologies than later years.
- Rankings are out of 191-193 UN Member States depending on the survey year.
