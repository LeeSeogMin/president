> Access date: 2026-03-26

Korea has ranked **1st** in the OECD Digital Government Index for three consecutive editions (2019 pilot, 2023, 2025), demonstrating sustained leadership in digital government transformation.

## 5. Key Findings

- Korea achieved a **perfect score of 1.00** in Data-Driven Public Sector in both 2023 and 2025 editions
- Korea's composite score improved by **+25.2%** from 2019 (0.747) to 2023 (0.935)
- Korea ranked **1st in 4 of 6 dimensions** in the 2023 DGI, and 2nd in the remaining 2
- The gap between Korea and the OECD average widened from 0.246 (2019) to 0.330 (2023) to 0.25 (2025)
- In 2023, Korea topped the list in: Data-Driven Public Sector, Government as a Platform, Open by Default, and Proactiveness

## Methodology Notes

- The DGI measures the extent to which governments have established the conditions and capabilities needed for a coherent and sustainable digital transformation of the public sector.
- It is based on the OECD Recommendation on Digital Government Strategies (2014) and the OECD Digital Government Policy Framework (DGPF).
- The index is composed of 6 equally-weighted dimensions, each scored on a 0-1 scale.
- The composite score is the average of the 6 dimension scores.
- Data is collected through an OECD survey of government officials, supplemented by desk research.
- The 2019 edition was a "pilot" and used a slightly different methodology; the 2023 and 2025 editions use a revised methodology for better comparability.
